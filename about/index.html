Two Factor Authentication

TwoFactorAuth.org is a website that lists a number of popular web services, and whether or not they support two-factor authentication. TwoFactorAuth.org aims to encourage the use of two-factor authentication by making it easier to track which websites use two-factor authentication, and how two-factor authentication can be enabled.

What is two-factor authentication?

Two-factor authentication is a web-oriented security technology that helps to ensure you, and only you, can access a particular web-based service. This is done by verifying you have access to some form of personal device before logging in. Two-factor authentication refers to the factors that are available to confirm your identity upon login. Typically, these factors include:

  1. Something you know, such as a password
  2. Something you have or own, such as a mobile phone
  3. and occasionally, something you are, such as a fingerprint

How do I use it?

Two-factor authentication can be set up with many of the online accounts you already use. All that is needed is a mobile device, such as a smartphone or tablet. Most web services allow you to begin setting up two-factor authentication from the account settings page. If you don't have a mobile device able to support a two-factor authentication app, separate hardware options are often available. What does it protect against?

A common misconception is that two-factor authentication is a catch-all solution to any security issues involving passwords. This is not the case. Two-factor authentication can help protect against a number of threats, but it is important to remain vigilant and take reasonable security precautions whenever possible. Here are some situations where two-factor authentication is very helpful:

  • I accidentally clicked on a phishing email, and somebody on the other side of the world is trying to login to my account with my password.
  • My favorite website didn't secure their databases well enough, and the contents of their databases have now been dumped online for anybody to see.
Here are some situations where two-factor authentication isn't so helpful:
  • One of my devices has a virus (Attackers that have access to your devices through a virus can find ways to bypass two-factor authentication).
  • I entered my two-factor authentication token into a sophisticated website that aims to fraudulently access my account.
